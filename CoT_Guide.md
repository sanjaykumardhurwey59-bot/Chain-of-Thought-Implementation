# Chain-of-Thought (CoT) Prompting Guide

## Introduction
Chain-of-Thought (CoT) prompting is a prompting technique used in Large Language Models (LLMs) where the model is encouraged to think step by step before generating the final answer.

This method improves logical reasoning and reduces mistakes in complex tasks.

---

## Basic CoT Prompt Example

```text
Let's think step by step.
```

This simple phrase helps the AI explain its reasoning process clearly.

---

## Why Use CoT Prompting?

- Improves logical reasoning
- Gives more accurate answers
- Helps solve multi-step problems
- Useful for mathematics and coding
- Makes AI responses easier to understand

---

## Applications of CoT Prompting

### 1. Mathematics
Example:
- Percentage problems
- Distance and speed calculations

### 2. Logical Reasoning
Example:
- Puzzle solving
- Analytical questions

### 3. Coding
Example:
- Explaining algorithms
- Debugging programs

### 4. Decision Making
Example:
- Comparing multiple solutions
- Stepwise analysis

---

## Zero-Shot CoT

Zero-Shot CoT means giving the AI a step-by-step instruction without providing examples.

Example:

```text
Let's think step by step.
```

The model automatically generates reasoning steps.

---

## Best Practices

- Use clear questions
- Ask the AI to explain reasoning
- Verify the final answer
- Break complex tasks into smaller parts

---

## Conclusion

Chain-of-Thought prompting is an effective technique to improve the reasoning ability of AI models. It helps generate more logical, transparent, and accurate responses for complex problems.
